Finding Mind: Why the Origin of Mind Requires Updating Our Definition of Computation

“Prolegomena to finding mind.” — Lucius Meredith (Feb 2026)

In brief: The project argues that telling a serious story about the origin of mind requires first updating the reader’s default story about what computation is. Information-theoretic approaches are showing up across domains: aging, black holes, AI cognition models. It reframes the hard problem of consciousness as ‘What is it like to be a computer program?’

Key takeaways

  • The project argues that telling a serious story about the origin of mind requires first updating the reader’s default story about what computation is.
  • Information-theoretic approaches are showing up across domains: aging, black holes, AI cognition models.
  • It reframes the hard problem of consciousness as ‘What is it like to be a computer program?’

Key questions

  • What definition of computation is rich enough to support a theory of mind?
  • How does the information-theoretic lens change what we build?
  • Is ‘what is it like to be a computer program?’ a tractable question?

Why it matters for developers

The models we choose for computation shape what kinds of minds and agents we believe are possible — and how we evaluate them.
